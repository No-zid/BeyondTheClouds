MERCURY

Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun and due to its proximity it is not easily seen except during twilight...

FACTS

  • Mercury does not have any moons or rings.
  • Mercury is the smallest planet.
  • Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun.
  • Your weight on Mercury would be 38% of your weight on Earth.
  • A solar day on the surface of Mercury lasts 176 Earth days.
  • A year on Mercury takes 88 Earth days.
  • It's not known who discovered Mercury.

VENUS

Venus is often called Earth's twin due to their similar size and mass. However, that's where the similarities end...

FACTS

  • Venus has no moons.
  • Venus is the hottest planet in our solar system.
  • Venus has a thick atmosphere composed mainly of carbon dioxide.
  • Your weight on Venus would be 91% of your weight on Earth.
  • A day on Venus lasts 243 Earth days.
  • A year on Venus takes 225 Earth days.
  • Venus is named after the Roman goddess of love and beauty.

EARTH

Earth is the only known planet in the universe that supports life. It's home to a diverse range of ecosystems and species...

FACTS

  • Earth has one moon.
  • Earth is the only planet in our solar system with liquid water.
  • Earth's atmosphere is composed of 78% nitrogen and 21% oxygen.
  • Your weight on Earth is, well, your weight!
  • A day on Earth lasts 24 hours.
  • A year on Earth takes 365.25 days.
  • Earth is the third planet from the Sun.

MARS

Mars is known as the Red Planet due to its reddish appearance, which is caused by iron oxide (rust) on its surface...

FACTS

  • Mars has two small moons, Phobos and Deimos.
  • Mars is home to the largest volcano in the solar system, Olympus Mons.
  • A day on Mars (called a sol) is about 24.6 hours.
  • A year on Mars takes 687 Earth days.
  • Mars has the largest dust storms in the solar system.
  • It is believed that Mars once had liquid water on its surface.

JUPITER

Jupiter is the largest planet in our solar system, known for its Great Red Spot, a giant storm that has been raging for centuries...

FACTS

  • Jupiter has 79 known moons, including the four largest: Io, Europa, Ganymede, and Callisto.
  • Jupiter is a gas giant, primarily composed of hydrogen and helium.
  • A day on Jupiter lasts about 10 hours.
  • A year on Jupiter takes about 12 Earth years.
  • Jupiter has a very strong magnetic field and radiation belts.
  • It is known for its distinctive bands of clouds and storms.

SATURN

Saturn is famous for its stunning rings, which are made up of ice and rock particles...

FACTS

  • Saturn has 83 known moons, with Titan being the largest.
  • Saturn is a gas giant, primarily composed of hydrogen and helium.
  • A day on Saturn lasts about 10.7 hours.
  • A year on Saturn takes about 29.5 Earth years.
  • Saturn's rings are the most extensive and complex in the solar system.
  • It has a very low density; it could float in water if there were a body large enough.

URANUS

Uranus is known for its unique blue color and its extreme tilt, which causes it to rotate on its side...

FACTS

  • Uranus has 27 known moons, with Titania and Oberon being the largest.
  • It is an ice giant, primarily composed of water, ammonia, and methane ices.
  • A day on Uranus lasts about 17.2 hours.
  • A year on Uranus takes about 84 Earth years.
  • Uranus has a faint ring system composed of ice particles and dust.
  • It was the first planet discovered with a telescope in 1781 by William Herschel.

NEPTUNE

Neptune is known for its deep blue color and strong winds, making it the windiest planet in our solar system...

FACTS

  • Neptune has 14 known moons, with Triton being the largest.
  • It is also an ice giant, similar to Uranus, with a composition of hydrogen, helium, and methane.
  • A day on Neptune lasts about 16 hours.
  • A year on Neptune takes about 165 Earth years.
  • Neptune has a faint ring system and is known for its dynamic weather patterns.
  • It was discovered in 1846, based on mathematical predictions rather than direct observation.
